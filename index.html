Part II — The Invention & How It Works

How Bitcoin Works

Proof‑of‑Work

  • Energy cost-anchors security
  • Simple to verify
  • Open competition
  • No single control
  • No censorship
  • No central point of failure

Hashrate and Difficulty

  • The difficulty adjusts to keep ~10‑minute blocks, no matter the hashrate.
  • Hashrate = total compute securing the network; higher hashrate → harder to attack.
  • One Zettahash is (1 sextillion = 10^21) 1,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 hashes per second.

Historical Bitcoin price chart

Savings curve

  • DCA in plain words: Buy a fixed amount of bitcoin on a schedule (weekly or monthly).
  • When the price dips you automatically buy more; when it rises you buy fewer.
  • Over time this averages your cost and reduces the need to time the market.

Example outcome: total invested $3,700 → current value $18,999 (≈ 413.5% return), holding ~0.154 BTC.
